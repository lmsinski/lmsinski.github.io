SpendNote


The proof of concept for SpendNote was developed from ethnographic research and observation on the difference between cash and cashless spending. We chose to investigate the problem of people going into debt through cashless means such as credit, debit, and online payments. Since there are dozens of robust software and applications for money tracking and bookkeeping on the market, we decided to work on a browser application, serving more as a real-time reminder to the user as they browse online retail sites. This idea was inspired by Honey, which brings coupons and deal codes to the consumers' attention as they shop online. SpendNote would collect spending amounts and keep data to create a history of spending habits that the user can review. The users would also be able to set notifications at certain spending thresholds to remind them to stop spending past certain amounts, helping them maintain their own decided budget.
