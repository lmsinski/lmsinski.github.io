Thesis: IncogInput


My thesis project brought together both my partners' and my interest of organizational design and business management and practices as an application of critical design. We started with heavy research into scholarly literature and found that we gravitated towards the topics of collaboration and the affects of hierarchy in organiations. Different types of businesses and organizations will each work with tailored hieracrchies, whether they are leaning more vertically or horizontally. We proposed that we could conceptualize an application that would track how hierarchical rank would affect how people in an organization talked to each other.

From this, we created incoginput, an application made for collaboration. It functions similarly to an anonymous message board, with each project serving as the topic for discussion. The idea would be that each user could express their feedback and ideas without feeling stress from higher management, and employees of all ranks could ask clarification questions without fear of judgement. This would also combat personal biases between workers. As a user, you would only be able to see your own profile with designated "rank" in a company--everyone else is unknown to you.

The application system itself would also track who commented on who, and, using Circos, would output circular diagrams to show the connections between employees. You can see if upper management makes the most number of comments, or if the middle level of the company is not providing enough feedback, all while being anonymous. Seeing the connections and feedback roads provides data that would allow the company to assess their communications and tailor the fit to what is best for them.
